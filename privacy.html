Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026 | QlipLab v0.1.19

Overview

QlipLab is a local-first clipboard manager. Your data stays on your device. We do not collect, transmit, or store your clipboard content on any external server.

Data We Access

Clipboard content

Text and images you copy are stored locally in a SQLite database on your device. This data never leaves your computer.

Secure vault data

Vault items are encrypted with AES-256-GCM using a password only you know. Encrypted data is stored locally.

App settings

Your preferences (theme, history limit, etc.) are stored locally on your device.

System Permissions

Accessibility

Required to paste clipboard content into other applications via simulated Cmd+V keystroke (CGEvent) and for snippet auto-expansion trigger detection. macOS will prompt you to grant this permission on first use. This is the same standard permission used by all clipboard managers.

Network (Optional)

Only used for optional features: AI text processing (your own API key), opt-in crash reporting, and manual issue reporting. No background network activity occurs.

Optional Data Collection

Crash reports (opt-in)

If you enable auto error reporting, we send crash details (error message, stack trace, app version, OS version) to help improve the app. No clipboard content, vault data, or personal information is included.

Manual feedback

When you submit an issue report, only the information you explicitly type is sent.

Data We Do NOT Collect

No analytics or usage tracking
No advertising identifiers
No clipboard content transmitted
No vault passwords or encrypted data
No personal information collected
No third-party tracking services

Data Storage

All data is stored locally in the app's sandboxed data directory. Clipboard history is stored in plain text in SQLite. Vault items are encrypted with AES-256-GCM before storage. You can delete all data at any time from within the app.

AI Features (Optional, User-Initiated)

QlipLab offers optional AI-powered text processing (summarize, translate, fix grammar, etc.). These features are disabled by default and require your own API key and explicit consent.

Data is sent directly from your device to the AI provider over encrypted HTTPS. QlipLab does NOT act as an intermediary.

QlipLab does NOT store, log, or have access to data sent to AI providers.

AI actions are automatically blocked for items detected as sensitive (passwords, API keys, credit cards).

Vault-encrypted data is never accessible to AI features.

No AI processing occurs without your explicit, manual action.

Third-Party Services

Crash reporting (opt-in)

Uses a secure endpoint to log crash reports. No clipboard or personal data is included.

AI providers (opt-in, user-initiated)

When you explicitly use AI features, your clipboard text is sent to Anthropic (api.anthropic.com) or OpenAI (api.openai.com) depending on your chosen provider. See their respective privacy policies.

No third-party analytics, advertising, or tracking services are used.
